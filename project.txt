──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE OFFER
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AI Vanguard — a small biweekly room of high-output operators who
advance the front line together. Invite-only. Nothing to pay, and
money can't get you in. The only currency is what you bring.

The unit metaphor: a Roman vanguard. The person at the front spends
themselves clearing path, building bridge, hacking through. As they
tire, the next one jumps in front. The column moves at a pace no lone
traveller could match.

Each person clears something — a setup, a skill, a shortcut — and
leaves the ladder in place for the rest of the group to climb up,
then bushwhacks forward from there.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
THE BIG REFRAMES (work into copy)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
1. WE ARE NOT PROGRAMMERS. We are architects directing AI employees.
   Most "anti-tech" entrepreneurs miss that the tech IS the business
   now. AI is your team — the question is how good a CEO you are.

2. THE WINDOW. This year is arbitrage — people still pay pre-AI
   prices for websites, apps, content, anything we can now ship in
   hours. Next year is the age of insane-custom impossible things,
   stuff nobody can even visualise yet. We want to be at the front
   of THAT wave, not catching up to this one.

3. THE REAL CURRENCY. Money filters lower-tier rooms. Time and energy
   filter higher-tier rooms. You're already wealthy in money compared
   to the people who'd pay $5k for a course — but your time and your
   creative energy are finite. So is ours. The question is whether
   this room earns those.

4. ALONE vs WITH THE COLUMN. Solo, getting your AI stack right is
   ~3 months of head-scratching. With the shortcuts already laid down
   by the group, a high-energy operator can have the base set up in
   a few days and be fully caught up in 1–2 weeks. Then they start
   bushwhacking in front for the rest.

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT THIS IS *NOT* (directly counter first-session confusion)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- NOT a general business mastermind
- NOT a place to lurk silently and absorb
- NOT for people who want to be told what to do
- NOT a sales pitch for a course or coaching upsell
- NOT vague — every session is concrete: this is the setup I'm
  running, this is what works, this is what failed, this is the
  prompt, this is the output

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
HOW A MEETING ACTUALLY RUNS
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
- Biweekly Zoom
- Show up 10–15 mins early to chat / catch up if you want
- 90 minutes locked-in focus — concrete sharing only
  (no chitchat during the locked block)
- Stay as long as you want after for hangout / deep dives
- Format: members show their actual setups, demos, prompts, skills
- Group picks the best, standardises, then explores the next thing

This means: a busy operator can show up, give and get 90 minutes
of dense value, leave on schedule. People who want more hangout
get it before or after.
