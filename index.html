The reframe almost everyone is missing

We are not programmers. We are architects directing AI employees.

A lot of entrepreneurs are still saying "I'm not into the tech, I'd rather focus on the business." That sentence used to make sense. It doesn't any more.

The tech is the business now. AI is your team. You're not sitting down to be a programmer — you're sitting down to brief the largest, most talented team you've ever managed, at a speed no human team could match. The question isn't whether you can code. It's whether you can describe what you want clearly and decide what good looks like.

That's the job. CEO of a hundred AI employees. Architect of a mission. If you can run a business, you can run this. The column is full of people teaching each other exactly how.

And to be very clear about what we teach each other: we don't learn AI for the sake of learning AI. The frontier moves every fortnight — chasing every new model and toy would burn your whole life. We hunt the small number of core skills, prompts and stacks that compound: things that take a couple of hours to install and then save you a couple of hours on every project you ship from then on. Maximum output for minimum input. The column filters what's worth your time so you don't have to.

Why we call it a vanguard

One clears the path. The next sprints through. Then bushwhacks ahead.

Think about how the Roman army actually moved through hostile country. The vanguard at the front spent themselves — felling trees, digging through, building bridges across rivers. Brutal, exhausting work. As one man tired, the next jumped in front. The column never slowed. The whole army arrived together, fast.

Now picture ten people learning AI alone. Ten machetes. Ten people hacking through the same wood, in parallel, in the dark, never seeing what anyone else found. Slow. Exhausting. Lonely. Stupid.

The column does the opposite. One member spends three weekends cracking a workflow, a stack, a skill — then drops the ladder for everyone else. The next member runs through the cleared path in hours instead of weeks and bushwhacks ahead. Then the one behind catches up and does the same for the next thing. Everyone moves at the front-line pace. Nobody walks alone.

First session, a few people showed up expecting one of these. They came away disappointed. We don't want that to happen to you, so here it is in plain English.

A general business mastermind.

This isn't "let's talk about funnels and offers and Q4 planning." It's specifically about being at the AI front line and bringing the rest of your business along with it.

Not

A place to watch from the side.

Members who only take and never share don't stay. Show your setup, show your prompts, show what failed. Everyone gives, or no one does.

A guru with followers.

There's no expert at the front. Anyone claiming to be the master of AI right now is selling something. We run experiments and compare notes — that's it.

No "I've been thinking about getting into AI." No "what should I learn first?" We share specific setups, specific prompts, specific outputs. The group picks the best and standardises on it.

The AI Mastermind itself is not paid. No upsell at the end of the 90 minutes, no "next level" you can buy your way into. The value comes from everyone in the column giving — not from someone selling you something off the back of the meeting.

Not

Endless chitchat.

The 90 minutes is locked focus — concrete sharing only. Early arrivals and late stayers can chat as long as they want. That structure is what protects busy entrepreneurs' time.
